What is a SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing in mutual funds where an investor chooses to invest a fixed amount at regular intervals. Unlike a lump sum investment, SIP helps you average out the cost of purchase and beat market volatility.

How does this SIP Calculator work?

This tool uses the compounding formula to predict your future wealth. It assumes that your monthly returns are reinvested. The formula used is:

FV = P × ({[1 + i]^n - 1} / i) × (1 + i)

Where 'P' is the amount you invest, 'n' is the number of payments, and 'i' is the periodic interest rate.

Benefits of starting early

The power of compounding works best with time. As seen in the calculator, investing for 10 years yields significantly higher returns than 5 years due to interest-on-interest accumulation. This is why financial experts recommend starting SIPs as early as possible.

Understanding Loan EMI

An Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

Factors affecting your EMI

Three main factors determine your monthly outflow:

  • Principal: The loan amount borrowed.
  • Interest Rate: Higher rates increase the EMI significantly.
  • Tenure: Longer tenure reduces monthly EMI but increases total interest paid.

How to reduce your Home Loan burden?

Use this calculator to test different scenarios. Often, making one extra EMI payment per year can reduce your loan tenure by several years and save lakhs in interest.

What is GST?

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a comprehensive, multi-stage, destination-based tax that is levied on every value addition in India. It has replaced many indirect taxes laws that previously existed in India.

Current GST Tax Slabs (2025)

In India, GST is categorized into four main slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Essential items usually fall under lower brackets, while luxury goods attract higher taxes.

Formula for Calculation

GST Amount = (Original Cost × GST Rate) / 100

Net Price = Original Cost + GST Amount
